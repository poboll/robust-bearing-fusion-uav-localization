# Journal Variants V1

## Variant A: Engineering Applications of Artificial Intelligence

### Title
Robust Collaborative Bearing-Only Passive Localization for UAV Swarms under Degraded Measurements

### Abstract
Passive bearing-only localization is an important capability for collaborative UAV systems operating in GNSS-degraded or electromagnetically silent environments. However, classical geometric and least-squares methods are highly sensitive to biased bearings, missing observations, and outlier corruption, while heuristic global-search solvers are often difficult to interpret and reproduce. This paper proposes a geometry-preserving robust passive localization framework that integrates consensus geometric initialization, trimming-aware residual filtering, and iterative reweighted refinement. The framework is designed to close the gap between lightweight geometric estimation and robust engineering-oriented decision making under degraded sensing. Extensive synthetic experiments under biased, outlier, and mixed corruption regimes show that the proposed method improves stability relative to least-squares baselines, strongly reduces large-error failures, and remains competitive with heuristic baselines at comparable runtime cost to PSO in the current implementation. Formation-generalization experiments further show that the method remains effective across circular, elliptical, perturbed, and random swarm layouts. The resulting system provides a reproducible and interpretable localization module suitable for intelligent collaborative UAV applications.

## Variant B: Applied Soft Computing

### Title
Geometry-Preserving Robust Passive Localization for Collaborative UAV Systems with Biased and Outlier-Corrupted Bearings

### Abstract
Collaborative UAV systems require passive localization methods that remain effective under uncertainty, corruption, and incomplete observations. This paper proposes a geometry-preserving robust bearing-only localization framework that combines consensus-style initialization with trimming-aware iterative reweighting for degraded measurement handling. The method is intended to preserve the interpretability of classical geometry while improving robustness under bias, outliers, and mixed degradation. Experiments under multiple corruption regimes show substantial gains over least-squares baselines in median accuracy and catastrophic failure control, while remaining competitive with representative heuristic search methods. Additional formation-generalization experiments show that the method is not restricted to a single idealized swarm layout. The proposed framework therefore offers a practical soft-computing style solution for uncertainty-tolerant collaborative UAV localization.

## Variant C: Expert Systems with Applications

### Title
An Interpretable Robust Passive Localization Framework for Collaborative UAV Systems under Degraded Bearing Measurements

### Abstract
Bearing-only passive localization is attractive for collaborative UAV systems operating in low-emission or GNSS-denied conditions, but classical estimators remain vulnerable to corrupted measurements. This paper presents an interpretable robust localization framework that combines consensus geometric initialization, trimming-aware residual suppression, and iterative reweighted refinement for degraded bearing-only estimation. The method is designed to bridge fragile classical geometry and less interpretable heuristic search by improving robustness while retaining transparent estimation logic. Experiments under biased, outlier, and mixed regimes show improved median accuracy, reduced catastrophic failures, and competitive performance relative to stochastic heuristic baselines. Additional formation-generalization and runtime experiments support the practical value of the proposed framework as an intelligent estimation component for collaborative UAV systems.
