What are the main findings?

- The proposed corruption-aware front end reduces the strict catastrophic-failure rate at $0.2R$ from 0.293 to 0.040 in the outlier regime and from 0.273 to 0.068 in the mixed regime relative to least squares.
- Across 536 measured-data replay windows and 227 deadline-filtered replay windows, the method does not claim universal median dominance but repeatedly contracts replay upper-tail localization and tracking errors in disturbed cases after stale or late bearings are discarded.

What are the implications of the main findings?

- A passive multi-UAV sensing stack can use the proposed method as a robust current-cycle front end to reduce catastrophic cue displacement before downstream tracking, handoff, or replanning.
- Fixed-budget screening should be treated as an optional engineering extension for bandwidth- or latency-constrained cycles; all-sensor robust fusion remains the default when the full bearing set is available.
