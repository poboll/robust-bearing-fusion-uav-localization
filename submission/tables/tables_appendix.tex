\section{Threats to Validity}

The most important construct-validity threat is that corrupted bearings are represented by parameterized stochastic models rather than by an onboard sensor stack. The pseudo-physical and PyBullet replay layers reduce this gap, but they do not remove it. The most important internal-validity threat is that the screening module remains an engineered score with fixed coefficients, although the exact $\pm20\%$ perturbation study shows that its behavior is not tied to one brittle weight vector. The most important external-validity threat is that the study remains planar and centralized. These limits define the current scope and motivate future extensions toward SITL, HITL, field-log replay, and full three-dimensional localization.

\section{Sketch of a 3D Extension}

The same front-end structure extends naturally to three-dimensional passive localization if each observer pose is represented as $s_i=[x_i,y_i,z_i]^T$ and the target state is represented as $p=[x,y,z]^T$. A compact measurement model is
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{z}_i=
\begin{bmatrix}
\psi_i\\
\varphi_i
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
\operatorname{atan2}(y-y_i,x-x_i)\\
\operatorname{atan2}\!\left(z-z_i,\sqrt{(x-x_i)^2+(y-y_i)^2}\right)
\end{bmatrix}
\mathbf{b}_i+\boldsymbol{\epsilon}_i+\mathbf{o}_i,
\end{equation}
where $\psi_i$ and $\varphi_i$ denote azimuth and elevation, respectively. The 3D front end would keep the same two-stage logic: consensus-style seeding, trimmed robust refinement, and optional budget-aware screening, but the Jacobian and FIM terms would become $3\times 3$ objects and the observability score would need to reflect both horizontal and vertical geometry. A distributed V2V version would then decide whether to transmit full angle packets, compressed sufficient statistics, or only screened subsets.
